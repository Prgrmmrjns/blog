Diabetes and metabolic diseases affect hundreds of millions of people. The combination of continuous glucose monitors (CGMs), wearables, and machine learning is opening new ways to understand and manage these conditions.

## The Data Revolution

We're no longer limited to sporadic clinic visits. CGMs stream glucose data every few minutes. Wearables capture heart rate, activity, and sleep. Smartphones log meals and medication. The challenge shifts from collecting data to making sense of it.

## Where ML Fits In

- **Time series forecasting:** Predicting glucose excursions before they happen, enabling proactive interventions.
- **Phenotyping:** Clustering patients by similar trajectories to personalize treatment.
- **Anomaly detection:** Flagging unusual patterns that might indicate acute risk or suboptimal control.
- **Natural language:** Extracting structured information from clinical notes and patient-reported outcomes.

## Challenges

Real-world data is messy. Missing values, irregular sampling, and heterogeneity across devices and populations are the norm. Models must be robust, interpretable, and aligned with clinical workflows—not just accurate on a leaderboard.

## Looking Ahead

Hybrid modeling—combining mechanistic (physiological) models with data-driven ML—is promising. We know a lot about glucose-insulin dynamics; ML can fill the gaps and capture individual variation. The goal: decision support that clinicians trust and patients can use.
